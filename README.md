# Geospatial Analysis Portfolio

This repository presents a collection of geospatial and remote sensing projects that demonstrate the application of satellite imagery and GIS-based spatial analysis and modelling for studying environmental change monitoring, natural hazards, and geological processes.

The projects included in this portfolio explore how geospatial technologies can be used to monitor ecosystems, analyze disaster events, and interpret landscape characteristics through satellite data. By combining remote sensing techniques with spatial analysis tools, the projects provide insights into environmental dynamics and spatial patterns in different regions.

---

## Objectives of the Portfolio

• To demonstrate practical applications of GIS and remote sensing in environmental and hazard analysis  
• To analyze satellite imagery using spectral indices and spatial modeling techniques  
• To perform temporal change detection using multi-date satellite data  
• To interpret spatial patterns related to vegetation, water bodies, geological formations, and natural hazards  

---

## Projects Included

### 1. Clay Mineral Mapping – Cuddalore, Tamil Nadu, India
This project focuses on identifying clay mineral distribution using Landsat satellite imagery and spectral index techniques. Clay minerals exhibit distinctive spectral responses in specific wavelength bands, allowing them to be detected through band ratio analysis.

The methodology involved preprocessing satellite imagery, masking non-target areas such as vegetation, water bodies, and built-up regions, and applying spectral ratio models to highlight clay-rich zones. The resulting maps illustrate potential areas of clay mineral concentration and demonstrate the effectiveness of remote sensing techniques in geological exploration.

---

### 2. Forest Fire Mapping – Los Angeles, USA
This project analyzes wildfire events using satellite data to identify fire hotspots and associated thermal patterns. The analysis focuses on the Eaton Fire and Palisades Fire events in Los Angeles.

Satellite imagery and MODIS fire datasets were used to detect active fire locations, while land surface temperature data helped identify thermal anomalies associated with fire activity. Spatial analysis techniques were applied to visualize fire distribution and affected regions and MODIS fire points and thermal datas(MOD21) are used to cross check the resulted analysis.

The results highlight the usefulness of remote sensing in monitoring wildfire events and supporting disaster management efforts.

---

### 3. Cyclone Impact Mapping – Odisha, India
This project investigates the spatial distribution of cyclone activity affecting the coastal region of Odisha. Historical cyclone track data were analyzed using GIS tools to identify areas frequently impacted by cyclone events.

Kernel Density analysis was applied to map regions with a higher concentration of cyclone activity. In addition, a temporal animation was created to visualize cyclone formation and movement across the Bay of Bengal over multiple years which can be helpful is analysisng and predicting future occurances and improve the disaster management activities in the frequently affected areas.

This project demonstrates how geospatial tools can help understand cyclone patterns and support disaster preparedness planning.

---

### 4. Tectonic Activity Ranking – Sirmaur, Himachal Pradesh, India
This study evaluates tectonic activity within the Sirmaur watershed region through geomorphological and spatial analysis. Various terrain characteristics and geomorphic indicators were examined to assess tectonic influence on the landscape mainly on the river course.

GIS-based analysis was used to compare these indicators and classify zones according to their relative tectonic activity levels. The results contribute to understanding regional geological processes and landscape evolution.

---

### 5. NDVI and MNDWI Temporal Analysis – Sundarbans
This project explores environmental change in the Sundarbans mangrove ecosystem using multi-temporal satellite imagery.

Two key spectral indices were applied:

NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) to assess vegetation density and health  
MNDWI (Modified Normalized Difference Water Index) to identify and monitor water bodies  

By comparing satellite images from different years, the analysis reveals patterns of vegetation change and variations in water distribution within the region. It is useful for policy making and sustainable environmental activities.

---

### 6. Forest Degradation Mapping – Sundarbans
This project further focuses on identifying areas experiencing forest degradation within the Sundarbans ecosystem.

Satellite-based vegetation indices were used to analyze changes in vegetation density over time. Areas showing significant decline in vegetation index values were interpreted as potential forest degradation zones.

The resulting maps provide insights into ecosystem changes and demonstrate the role of remote sensing in monitoring forest health and supporting conservation efforts.

## Tools and Techniques Used

• GIS Spatial Analysis  
• Remote Sensing Image Processing

Software and data sources used in the projects include GIS platforms(ARCPRO, ARC GIS DESKTOP, ERDAS IMAGINE), satellite imagery datasets, and spatial analysis tools commonly applied in geospatial research.
